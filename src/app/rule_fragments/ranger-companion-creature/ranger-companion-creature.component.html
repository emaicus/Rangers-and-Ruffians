Taking a Companion Creature

When you take the Companion Creature ability, you may choose an animal companion to aid you in your travels. Select a tier appropriate creature from the list of known monsters, or work with your Poohbah to develop a new one. Creatures that are available as companions are marked with the word "companion" after their type. For the remainder of your journey, you will form a bond with this creature and it will serve at your side both in combat and while adventuring.

Changing Gameplay Tiers

When you change gameplay tiers, your companion grows stronger. Daggerfin Fries become Daggerfin. Dire Bears become Ursa. As your companion grows alongside you, it will become more dangerous and more helpful to you both in combat and out.

Fighting Alongside a Companion

When in combat, your companion moves on the same initiative as you, and its turn occurs at the same time as yours. Any spell power saves which could result in one of you shifting in initiative order are made by you and affect you both. When your companion creature reaches one quarter health, it will become panicked. Roll against the panicked table to determine the outcome. The panicked condition ends once your companion is healed or you have spent a full turn calming it down.

Panicked Table
1. Escape. The creature runs as far as it can away from combat.
2. Rampage. The creature moves as far as it can towards the nearest combatant and attacks them.
3. Lock up. The creature locks up, and loses its turn.
4. Hide. The creature runs as far as it can towards you, and tries to hide behind you.
5-6. Well Trained. The creature remembers its training, and remains under your control.
Adventuring with a Companion

As you level up, you can train your companion to perform tasks for you. Once you have trained your companion, it is able to perform the task reliably, though a check may still have to be made for it to succeed. For such checks, add your expertise to the result. Whenever you wish for your companion to perform a task for which it has not been trained, you must make a Companion Creature Check set by your Grand Poohbah. Roll 1d20 and add your expertise. On success, your creature is able to carry out the task. On failure, Something Bad Happens.
